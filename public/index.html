What's a SneakyNote?

A SneakyNote is a special URL.

It can only be read once.

It only lasts for 10 minutes.

It's encrypted on your computer and decrypted on your friend's computer.

SneakyNote.com must store your encrypted message briefly, but we never see the decryption key. We can't read your message.

We make sure your message never touches a hard disk. And when a SneakyNote is read or expires, we thoroughly erase it from memory.

Because a SneakyNote can only be read once, it is safe to email a SneakyNote URL to a friend. If they can read your message, they are the only person who has seen your message. As soon as they read it, no one else can ever access it again. It's gone.

Fast & Easy

Even though there are good cryptosystems like PGP, S/MIME, and even Apple's iMessages, if your recipient doesn't already have one of them set up then you can't securely send them a message.

A SneakyNote is just a URL. Instead of painfully setting up one of those services to send a short message, send your SneakyNote URL over plain email or SMS.

Verified Delivery

When your friend opens the message, you will both be presented with a short code. If you see the same code, your message arrived safely.

No Covert Interception

If you send your SneakyNote URL over an insecure channel, it is unlikely but possible for an attacker between you and your friend to read your note before your friend does. This is okay if you are prepared.

Always wait until your friend receives the SneakyNote before actually setting the password on any services.

A SneakyNote URL can only be read once. If the attacker reads the SneakyNote then your friend will not be able to see it.

If an attacker tries to get around that by substituting their own new SneakyNote URL, then when your friend opens the URL from the attacker they won't see the same verification code that you see. You should always check the verification code over the telephone.

In any case, you will know if someone intercepts your SneakyNote. If it happens, just make a new password and send a SneakyNote a different way.
